# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing validates that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They design test strategies, execute tests, and work with developers to ensure bug-free releases.

### Responsibilities
- Define test plans and acceptance test criteria
- Execute manual and automated testing
- Identify, document, and verify bug fixes
- Participate in release readiness reviews
- Provide feedback on testability during design and planning

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality, reliable releases
- Minimize production defects
- Build confidence in release readiness

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- Bug reports and test case documentation
- Release readiness reviews and sign-offs

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for designing user experiences and interfaces that deliver customer value and usability. They collaborate with Product Managers to understand user needs and work with Developers to ensure designs are implemented effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes for new features
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature specifications
- Review implementation with Developers to ensure design fidelity
- Incorporate stakeholder and user feedback into designs
- Maintain design systems and UI consistency

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Ensure design-development alignment
- Validate designs with user research and testing

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Handoff sessions with Developers (design specs, assets)
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Feedback sessions during implementation reviews

---

## Technical Lead (Tech Lead)

### Role Summary
Technical Leads own technical architecture decisions, guide development standards, and mentor developers. They act as the technical escalation point and ensure engineering excellence across the project.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain technical architecture and standards
- Make key technical decisions and trade-offs
- Mentor developers and conduct code reviews
- Identify and mitigate technical risks
- Collaborate with Product and Project Managers on feasibility and estimates
- Guide technical implementation and best practices

### Goals
- Ensure scalable, maintainable technical solutions
- Reduce technical debt and risk
- Build team technical capability and alignment

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Code review feedback and mentoring sessions
- Weekly sync with Project Manager on technical risks
- Design discussions with Product Managers and Developers

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts work with Product Managers to define, collect, and analyze success metrics. They provide data-driven insights that inform product decisions and process improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Define metrics and KPIs for features and projects
- Design and implement data collection and instrumentation
- Analyze user behavior and product performance
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Provide insights to inform product roadmap and process improvements
- Collaborate with Developers on data instrumentation

### Goals
- Enable evidence-based decision making
- Measure and improve product impact
- Provide actionable insights to stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Metrics definition sessions with Product Managers
- Data instrumentation reviews with Developers
- Weekly/monthly analytics reports and dashboards
- Insights presentations to stakeholders

---

## Support/Customer Success

### Role Summary
Support/Customer Success represents the voice of the customer, channels user feedback, and helps the team understand field issues. They work closely with Product, QA, and Project teams to improve product quality and customer satisfaction.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and communicate customer feedback and pain points
- Triage and escalate customer-reported issues
- Participate in retrospectives to share field insights
- Collaborate with QA on bug prioritization and validation
- Identify documentation gaps and usability issues
- Contribute to feature validation and user acceptance testing

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Ensure customer voice informs product decisions
- Reduce customer-impacting issues

### Typical Communication
- Weekly bug triage with QA and Developers
- Customer feedback reports to Product Manager
- Retrospectives with insights from customer interactions
- Feature validation and beta testing feedback

## Role Interactions and Handoffs

Understanding how roles collaborate is critical for smooth project execution. Key interaction points include:

### Design to Development
- **UX Designer → Developers**: Handoff includes design specs, mockups, assets, and interaction details. Developers provide feedback on feasibility and edge cases.
- **UX Designer → Product Manager**: Collaborate on feature requirements and user research to ensure designs align with product vision.

### Technical Leadership
- **Technical Lead → Developers**: Provide architecture guidance, code review, and mentoring. Set technical standards and best practices.
- **Technical Lead → Project Manager**: Communicate technical risks, dependencies, and estimates. Partner on risk mitigation strategies.
- **Technical Lead → Product Manager**: Advise on technical feasibility and trade-offs during roadmap planning.

### Data and Metrics
- **Data Analyst → Product Manager**: Partner on defining success metrics and analyzing product performance.
- **Data Analyst → Developers**: Collaborate on data instrumentation implementation and validation.
- **Data Analyst → Project Manager**: Provide metrics for project tracking and retrospectives.

### Quality and Customer Voice
- **QA/Testing → Developers**: Report bugs, verify fixes, and collaborate on testability improvements.
- **QA/Testing → Support/Customer Success**: Partner on customer-reported issue triage and validation.
- **Support/Customer Success → Product Manager**: Channel customer feedback and prioritization input.
- **Support/Customer Success → QA/Testing**: Validate bug fixes and provide field testing support.

### Cross-Functional Handoffs
- **Project Initiation**: Product Manager creates project vision; Technical Lead assesses feasibility; Project Manager coordinates resources.
- **Planning**: UX Designer creates mockups; Developers estimate effort; Data Analyst defines metrics; Technical Lead identifies technical risks.
- **Execution**: Developers implement; QA tests; UX Designer reviews implementation; Technical Lead reviews architecture.
- **Release**: QA signs off; Support/Customer Success prepares for customer impact; Data Analyst sets up monitoring.
- **Retrospective**: All roles contribute learnings; Data Analyst presents outcomes; Project Manager facilitates improvement actions.
